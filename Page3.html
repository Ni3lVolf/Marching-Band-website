    • 2. Uniform & Dress Code:

    When wearing the Yellow Jacket regiment unifor, you represent our entire school and community. Meaning whilst in unifrom you are a reflection of School and this Program

  • Rehearsal: Athletic shoes (NO sandals/boots), breathable clothing, and your DotBook/music.
  • Performance: Shako, Long black socks, clean marching shoes (dinkles), No visible jewlery or long hair down.

    • 3. Grading & Eligibility

    Marching Band is a class! Your grade is determined by these key factors:

    Catagory Percentage Of Grade Description
    Participation 30% Participation means showing up to class, and taking part in the lesson. Doing the work, and putting in the effort. Those who fail to do so will receive no points for that day. Attendance for competitions on saturdays are mandatory and part of the grade. Missing them will affect their grade. The same is true fro football games and parades. (some events will be optional and labeled as such)
    Play checks (Scales/chords/Pieces) 30% Throughout the school year, students will be called up to perform for me. They will be asked to show me their understanding of scales, and pieces being studied. Showing understanding will provide a passing score; failing to show understanding will not. Students who fail these play checks are allowed to retake the play check as many times as they would like to make up points. I want you to be able to do it, even if it might take you longer than the time I allot.
    Music Theory Assignments 5% Throughout the year, we will have days set aside to work on music theory assignments. Knowing how to play is great, but a greater understanding of why you are doing what you are doing will only help you be even better.
    Music Theory Exams 5% Quizzes will be worth 10 points. Students will be allowed one (1) retake for each quiz within one (1) week of the original test day for potential full credit. After, it will max out at 70%.
    Performance Exams 30% There will be three concert performances throughout the year. The students will be graded based on their attendance and participation in those performances.

    Grading Scale:

    A =-------------------90%-100%

    B =-------------------80%-89%

    C =-------------------70%-79%

    D =-------------------60%-69%

    F =-------------------59% and below

    4. Classroom Rules

    1. Follow directions
    2. Treat the instruments with respect!
    3. Must be in class working
    4. NO food, drinks or gum (only water is permitted)
    5. Treat the teacher and other students with respect
    6. Do not interrupt the teacher or play your instrument while he is talking/playing for the class
    7. Help keep the room clean
    8. Phones must be put away!
    9. Students are not allowed to use teh restrooms in teh building, they must go to one outside
    10. May not be in other there rooms without adult presents

    The breaking of any of these rules will result in a warning, followed by a referral and a call to the parent/gaurdian of the student.If you have been assigned an instrument from the school, rtreat said instruments with respect. If it is damaged due to negligence you may have those privliges revoked.
