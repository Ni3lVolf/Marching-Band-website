Yellow Jacket Regiment Band Handbook/Dictionary

Welcome to the season! Please review our offical polocies, and review some phrases you might hear throughout the year.

1. Uniform items definition

Shako - Marching Band uniform hat


Gauntlets - Wrist and lower arm covering that is part of the Marching Band/Gaurd uniform


Plumes - They are very fragile fathers on the Marching Band uniform hats.(DO NOT TOUCH WITH BARE HANDS, OILS MIX AND CAUSE DISCOLORATION)

3. Drill & field Formations

Alignment - straight lines in ranks, files and diagonals

cleaning - Slang term that means to make each movement well defined and precies. Each has a definite point where the movement starts, chaneges and stops

Column - Two or more people staning behind one another

Company Front - A formation where the entire band or corps is in one large line, marching side by side

Contraction - Movement which produces smaller intervals between members

Cover - straight line in a colomn or file aligned on the front person

Cut Off - A signal that tells the band or corps to stop playing

Decrease front - A command that tells thte band to reduce the width of the band by reducing the distance between files

Distance - Spacing between individuals front to back

Down beat - A musical term used in conducting to identify the first beat of a measure of music

Execution - How well or precisely something is done This is often a key part of a judges evaluation for the preformance

Expansion - Movement which produces larger intervals between members

File - Two or more people standing behind one another

Follow the leader - Movement including a lead preformer marching a specified patter with the remaining performers following the same path.

Increase Front - A command that tells the band to increase the width of the band by increasing the distance between files

Position of ready - A slight pause before the preparatory bea in conducting. This allows the players to get set and watch the drum major or conductor

Drill - The steos and positions that make up the marching band show

Sets - The formations and band makes. students receive drill charts to provide them a location on the field relative to yard linee, sidekines, ect.

Drill Chart - Charts out the position of the entire band in different sets of the music

Dot sheet - Sheet that gives your individual field positions. You will be given this in band camp and taught how to interpret it.

Run through - Practicing the entire Marching Band show at once.(pro tip one more run through means about 2-3 more)

Cadence - The beat played by percussion during a parade to indicate the marching pace. Also played as the band marches off of the field after a show, rehearsal, or competition.

Step Off - The precise moment when a Marching Band parade performance starts.

4. Leadership & Structure

Drum Major(s) - Student conductor(s) who direct the marching band as it plays. Must audition.(for our band they will be classified as central and rear DM(drum Major)

Captain - A captain is typically in charge of a who part of the band such as woodwind captain and brass captain(mainly used for smaller bands)

Section leader - Student who is in charge of a Marching Band instrumnt section such as alto sax section leader. Must Apply

Section - Students who play the same instruments, i.e. the trumpet section.

5. Instrument sections & personnel

winds - The non-percussion part of the band; any instrument that is played by blowing air; includes both brass and woodwinds.

Drumline - The entire percussion section (battery and PIT).

Battery - Marching Band percussion section that carries drums and marches. Comprised of snare drums, bass drums, and quads (see Quads). Must audition.

Front Ensemble - Consists of keyboard instruments and auxiliary percussion. Also known as PIT (see PIT).

Percussion Instrument Team AKA PIT - Percussion section of a Marching Band that does not march. They are staged at the front of the band during shows and competitions. Comprised of xylophones, electric pianos, gongs, timpani, etc.

Quads AKA Tenors - Set of 4-6 connected drums played by a member of the Battery. Think of this as a drum set that you carry. Must audition.

Show Structure, Logistics & Rewards

Movement - Songs a Marching Band plays. Our shows usually have 3 movements designated as Movement 1, Movement 2, and Movement 3.

Sectional - A practice or rehearsal by only a section of instruments

Podium - Raised platform where the Drum Majors stand. Moved by the PIT Crew.

PIT Crew - Volunteers who help move, load, assemble, and push the PIT instruments onto the field for games and competitions. Pit crew members wear the same “uniform” shirt which can be purchased here.

Caption Awards - Awards marching bands win at competitions. Can include: Best drum majors, best percussion, best auxiliary, best music, best marching, best general effect, best visual effect, best pit crew, etc.
