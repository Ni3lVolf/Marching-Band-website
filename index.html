The Colton Yellow Jacket HighSchool Marching Band program has been providing music insight to students since 1895.

The Colton High School Yellowjacket Regiment is one of the longest-standing student organizations on campus, with a tradition that stretches back nearly as far as the school itself. The program provides students with opportunities to develop musicianship, leadership, discipline, teamwork, and school pride through both performance and competition. The Colton High School Yellowjacket Regiment is one of the longest-standing student organizations on campus, with a tradition that stretches back nearly as far as the school itself. The program provides students with opportunities to develop musicianship, leadership, discipline, teamwork, and school pride through both performance and competition. Beyond the music, the program acts as a leadership incubator where students learn responsibility and collaboration, supported heavily by family-driven booster activities and fundraisers like car washes. Ultimately, the band remains a vital part of Yellowjacket culture, helping members build lifelong friendships while representing the Colton community with pride.

As someone already involved in the program, I'd say one of its strongest characteristics is that it gives students significant leadership opportunities. Student leaders often help teach marching fundamentals, mentor younger members, organize sections, and contribute directly to the band's culture and success. This makes the program a strong environment for developing leadership skills that transfer beyond music.
