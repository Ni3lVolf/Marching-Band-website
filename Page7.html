Yellow Jacket Regiment Band Handbook/Dictionary

Welcome to the season! Please review our offical polocies, and some commands you should be hearing year round

Whistle Commands (for Drum Majors)

We will use whistle commands for two purposes

When battery is playing a street cadence a long whistle will tell them to finish set street cadence and go back to Marching Cadence

During Marching Cadence a long whistle tells the ensemble to get their instruments up and get ready to play whatever piece has been aligned for them to play during the parade

Voice commands

As You Were - Oral command that tells the group: cancel the last command

At Ease - Oral command that tells the group: keep the right foot planted and remain silent. Other moement is allowed

Atten-hut/attention - Oral command that tells the group: Go to attention.

Backwards March - Oral command that tells the group: Begin marching in the direction of your back.

By The Numbers - Oral command that tells the group: Do not do the command until I call each execution number. Example, "Right Face...One!" (band turns)... "Two!" (heels come together). This continues until the command, "Cancel By The Numbers" is given.

Counter March - A precise drill that has the band turn, rank by rank, and march the other direction.

Cover Down - Oral command that tells the group: Straighten the column or file.

Detail Halt - Oral command that tells the group: Stop marching.Diagonals: The 45 degree lines established by the band block.

Dismissed - Oral command that tells the group: They are released from the rehearsal or drill.

Double Time - A step where the band members moves at twice the speed of the music or cadence. It is also is a command that tells the group to move at twice their current tempo.

Dress left/set/right Dress - Oral command that tells the group: Straighten the rank to the center person. Usually the instruments are raised to the playing position, and each person's head snaps towards the alignment point. This may be done in either one or two counts. Members then dress the rank. The command is called, "Dress - set - Dress." To return to their original position, "Ready - Front" is called.

Forward March - the command that tells the froup: begin marching forward

to the Left/right Flank March - A movement that has the band execute a 90 degree turn to the left while on the march. The command is called; "By the Left Flank.....March !" (or Hut!)

Mark Time March - Oral command that tells the group: Begin marching in place.

Left/Right Oblique - 45 degree movement - half of a right or left flank.

Parade Rest - A relaxed position of attention. Usually done with left foot moving about 18 inches to the left. Instrument/hand positions vary between bands.

Six to five - Marching with a stride size that takes six steps to every 5 yard, slide marching: A style of marching where the band member marches in one direction and twist the upper body so they can play another

To The Rear Hut - Oral command that tells the group: Turn around and march in the opposite direction.
